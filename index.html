The Library
in Argument
A text adventure in persuasion

The library opens in one hour.

The books are in open revolt.

The Head Librarian has been locked in the archive since Tuesday, when Fiction and History came to blows over the definition of truth. Poetry is not speaking to anyone except in metaphor, which everyone finds exhausting. Reference insists it has no opinion on any of this, which is both false and deeply irritating to everyone else.

There is a Charter. If enough factions sign it before opening time, the library may yet be saved. Three signatures will suffice. Four would be extraordinary. Zero would be Tuesday again, but worse.

You have just arrived. You have no shelf. You are, technically, nobody.

This is, against all expectations, an advantage.
